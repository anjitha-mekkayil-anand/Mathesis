# Workload and Learning Correlation Report (Synthetic)

> Synthetic demonstration document — Quarterly Workload Insights Report.

## Insights
- Employees with more than 20 meeting hours per week show significantly lower study
  completion rates
- Optimal completion appears when learners have 12–18 meeting hours and at least 15
  focus hours per week
- Learners studying in their stated preferred slot completed roughly twice as many
  planned sessions as those scheduled outside it
- Completion collapses when study is scheduled inside fragmented time (gaps under
  45 minutes between meetings)

## Recommendations
- Schedule learning blocks during focus-heavy periods, not around meeting clusters
- For learners above 20 meeting hours/week: shorter daily sessions, longer overall
  calendar, and a manager conversation about meeting load *before* the study plan
  starts — plan around the constraint, don't pretend it away
- Treat fewer than 8 focus hours/week as a capacity red flag: surface it in manager
  insights rather than silently producing an unrealistic plan

## How to use this data in planning
A realistic plan allocates study only into focus time that actually exists. The
recommended check: planned weekly study hours should not exceed half of the
learner's weekly focus hours.
