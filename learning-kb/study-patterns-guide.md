# Recommended Study Patterns (Synthetic)

> Synthetic demonstration document from the internal Learning & Development Playbook.

## The pattern that works
- **1–2 hours of daily focused study** beats weekend cramming — retention drops
  sharply with sessions over 3 hours
- **Weekly assessment checkpoints** — a short practice set every week, not one big
  mock at the end
- **Target 75% practice score before booking** — internal pass rates fall steeply
  below this line
- Schedule sessions in the learner's preferred slot (morning/afternoon/evening) —
  adherence roughly doubles when the slot matches stated preference

## Sequencing rules
1. Start with the heaviest-weighted domain while energy is highest
2. Schedule the learner's weakest domain (self-rating ≤ 3) in week one AND again in
   the final week — first to surface gaps, last to consolidate
3. Alternate reading and lab days; never two pure-reading days in a row
4. Reserve the final week for practice assessments only — no new content

## Workload adaptation
- Learners with more than 20 meeting hours per week should plan half-length sessions
  (45–60 min) and extend the calendar, not the day
- Protect at least 3 focus blocks per week for study; fewer than that, recommend
  deferring the certification window rather than planning to fail
- Never schedule study against the learner's heaviest meeting day

## Milestones template
- Week 1: heaviest domain + weakest domain surfaced
- Mid-point: practice score ≥ 60%
- Final week: practice score ≥ 75% on two consecutive checkpoints → book the exam
