# Quarterly Team Learning Performance Summary (Synthetic)

> Synthetic demonstration document.

## Headline figures (last quarter, synthetic cohort)
- Average study time: 21 hours
- Practice score average at booking: 74%
- Pass rate: 68%

## Observations
- Learners with more than 20 study hours AND more than 75% practice scores show
  markedly stronger certification outcomes — both conditions together, not either alone
- Learners who booked below a 70% practice score failed in the clear majority of cases
- Fail-then-retry learners passed at a high rate on second attempt when they added
  focused hours on their weakest domain rather than re-studying everything
- Teams with manager-reviewed study plans completed significantly more sessions than
  teams where plans were self-managed only

## Risk patterns for manager attention
- A learner whose practice score plateaus for two consecutive weeks usually has a
  domain-level gap, not an effort gap — reassess domains before adding hours
- Capacity-constrained learners (heavy meeting load) who fall one week behind rarely
  recover without a schedule change — intervene at one week, not three
- "Quiet" learners with no logged sessions for 10+ days are the highest-risk group
  in the cohort

## Use in readiness decisions
Book when: practice ≥ 75% sustained + recommended hours met + no weak domain left
unaddressed. Recommend deferral otherwise — a failed exam costs more morale than a
four-week delay.
